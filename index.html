Introduction
Introduction

Work ethic is a set of values centered on discipline, hard work, and integrity in the workplace.

It defines how individuals approach their duties and responsibilities.

Strong work ethic contributes to personal growth and organizational success.

Example: Arriving on time shows respect for company policies.
  • Discipline
  • Integrity
  • Reliability
Professional Conduct

Professional conduct encompasses behavior, attitude, and etiquette in the workplace.

Key aspects include respect for others and adherence to workplace policies.

Maintaining professionalism fosters a positive work environment.

Code Example: Always use polite language in emails.
  • Respect colleagues
  • Follow the dress code
  • Communicate effectively
Time Management

Time management is critical for efficiency and productivity.

Prioritize tasks and allocate time effectively.

Use tools like calendars and task management apps to stay organized.

Tip: Use the 80/20 rule to focus on impactful tasks.
  • Set clear deadlines
  • Avoid procrastination
  • Plan daily schedules
Collaboration

Collaboration involves teamwork and open communication.

It is essential for achieving common goals and fostering innovation.

Respect diverse perspectives and contribute constructively.

Tip: Active listening enhances collaboration.
  • Participate actively
  • Be open to feedback
  • Share knowledge
Resignation Etiquette

Resigning from a job should be done professionally and respectfully.

Provide adequate notice as per company policy.

Express gratitude for the opportunity and offer to assist with the transition.

Example: Submit a resignation letter in writing.
  • Give proper notice
  • Complete pending tasks
  • Maintain professionalism
